## Presentation Time
- Oral Presentations: 7 minutes (no Q&A, including transition)
- Invited Talks: 5 minutes (no Q&A, including transition)

## Presentation Preparation
- Please bring your own PC for the presentation.
- On the day of your presentation, please inform the session chair of your attendance before the session begins.
- The aspect ratio of the projector in the main hall is 16:9.
- The available connection port for the projector is **HDMI** only. If your PC only has a USB Type-C port, please bring an adapter.
- You may use the MIRU Logo on your presentation slides. Click [here](/en/organization/logo) for more info.

## Presentation Procedure
- Once the presentation two slots before yours begins, please proceed to the waiting seats at the front of the audience (left for odd-numbered presentations, right for even-numbered presentations).
- Podiums are set up on both sides of the stage. When the previous presentation begins, please come up onto the stage and connect your screen. The operator will give a signal once the connection is successfully established.
- When the previous presentation is about to end, please prepare to go on stage. The displayed screen will be switched to yours as soon as the previous presentation finishes.

## Monitor Connection Test
Connection tests will be available before the program begins each day and during the lunch break. Presenters are requested to complete a connection test before their presentation. You may participate in a connection test on a day other than the day of your presentation.

## Interactive Presentation
All presenters of oral presentations and invited talks are also asked to present in the interactive session.  
Please refer to the guidelines for interactive presentations [here](/en/author/interactive).

## Recording of Presentations
At MIRU2026, oral presentations will be recorded for future administrative purposes. Please note that these recordings will not be made available to participants.
